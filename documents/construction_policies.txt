CONSTRUCTION PROJECT POLICIES

1. PROJECT DELAY MANAGEMENT
Construction projects may experience delays due to various factors including weather conditions, material shortages, labor availability, permit approvals, and unexpected site conditions. All delays must be documented within 24 hours of occurrence. Project managers are required to submit delay reports to stakeholders weekly.

2. SAFETY PROTOCOLS
All construction sites must comply with OSHA regulations. Hard hats, safety vests, and steel-toed boots are mandatory. Daily safety briefings are required before work begins. Any safety incidents must be reported immediately to the site supervisor and safety officer.

3. MATERIAL PROCUREMENT
Materials must be ordered at least 2 weeks in advance of required date. Approved vendors must be used for all purchases exceeding $5,000. Material quality inspections are mandatory upon delivery. Defective materials must be returned within 48 hours.

4. BUDGET AND COST MANAGEMENT
All project expenses must be tracked in the project management system. Budget overruns exceeding 10% require approval from the project director. Change orders must be approved before work begins. Monthly budget reviews are mandatory.

5. QUALITY ASSURANCE
Quality inspections must be conducted at each major milestone. Defective work must be corrected before proceeding to the next phase. Quality control checklists must be completed and signed by the site supervisor. Third-party inspections may be required for structural elements.
